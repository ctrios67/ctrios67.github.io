Global Sales Manager Dashboard

Introduction:

This is my first personal project that I have ever done (1st time with SiSense & 1st time with MSSQL). It is a dashboard containing 5 widgets, envisioned for a Global Sales Manager.

Tools Used:
  • BI Tool: SiSense
  • SQL: TSQL via SQL Server 2012 Express
  • Database (Sample): Northwind

1.) Customers per Country



This paints a picture of how sales throughout the globe are for countries. We have the number of customers per country given by the size of the circles.

  • Green indicates sales are above the total company average.
  • Yellowrepresents below the average.

Yellow doesn't necessarily signify failure, it's a point of interest, as there will always be a yellow circle. Clicking on the circles shows how many customers exist in that country.

Answers the following questions:

  • What countries lead sales?
  • How many customers are located in (country)?
  • Is there anything to improve upon (country)'s sales?

2.) Supplier In Stock vs. On Order


This here shows the user whether demand is being met by suppliers and their location around the world.

  • Suppliers not meeting stock are in Red.
  • Suppliers having zero stock is in yellow (again not necessarily bad, just a point of interest).
  • Suppliers meeting or exceeding demand is in green.

Answers the following questions:

  • What suppliers are meeting or exceeding demand?
  • What suppliers are not meeting demand?
  • Is there anything to be done with suppliers having 0 orders on demand?

3.) Total Sales by American Customers


Shown here are sales leaders in North and South America along with how diversified a client's Northwind product purchases are. This brings customer locations by city, showing where they reside and how big of a presence they have in that market. Size of the circle indicates sales.

  • Green indicates they purchase many unique products offered.
  • Gray is an average amount.
  • Red is a small number of unique purchases.

Answers the following questions:

  • Which customers are best sellers?
  • Could high sales of one customer affect nearby customers with lower sales count?
  • Should we explore customers who buy a low amount of our different products having a need for others?
  • Are supplier issues playing any part in low sales?

4.) Total Sales by European Customers


Similar to the American customer widget except in Europe.

Answers the following questions:

  • Which customers are best sellers?
  • Could high sales of one customer affect nearby customers with lower sales count?
  • Should we explore customers who buy a low amount of our different products having a need for others?
  • Why are many customers not buying any of our other products?
  • Are supplier issues playing any part in low sales?
