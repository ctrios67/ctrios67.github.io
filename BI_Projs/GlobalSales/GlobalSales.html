Global Sales Manager Dashboard

Introduction:

This is my first personal project. It is a dashboard containing 5 widgets, envisioned for a Global Sales Manager. On this page you will find the dashboard widgets in order of appearance, a description of what tables and columns were used, an explanation of what you are looking at, and a list of business questions they answer.

Note: The widgets with maps are interactive and tell more information when a circle is clicked. I (unfortunately) did not manage to capture of photo of this during my free trail of this software.

Tools Used:
  • BI Tool: SiSense
  • SQL: TSQL via SQL Server 2012 Express
  • Database (Sample): Northwind
Custom Tables Created:
  • Americas_Customer: The rationale behind the creation is that I realized that including City, Region, and Country from Customers created anomalies or missing data from widgets due to data conflicts. Resolved by realizing Region mainly reflected most of North America and South America. Argentina and Mexico were still missing though, so I adjusted the WHERE clause accordingly.
  • Europe_Customers: Realized that wherever Region was Null, the customer was likely in Europe, so I just excluded entries where Country was Mexico and Argentina, and now we have a table with every European customer.

1.) Customers per Country Measuring Total Sales


This dashboard widget uses:

  • Orders->ShipCountry
  • Orders->Subtotal
  • Orders->CustomerID
  • [Order Details]->OrderID

This paints a picture of how sales throughout the globe are for countries. We have the number of customers per country given by the size of the circles, green indicates sales are above the total company average, yellow represents below the average. Yellow doesn't necessarily signify failure, it's a point of interest, over time the user will be familiar to the slower selling countries and choose to be content with their sales or not. Clicking on the circles shows how many customers exist in that country.

Answers the following questions:

  • What countries lead sales?
  • How many customers are located in (country)?
  • Is there anything to improve upon (country)'s sales?

2.) Category Sales 97 vs. 98


This dashboard widget uses:

  • [Sales by Category]->CategoryName
  • [Order Subtotals]->Subtotals
  • Orders->ShippedDate

Shows how sales by category are trending from 1997 to 1998. In retrospect I should've switched the axes for readibility. We see a downward trend here, and in the next few dashboard widgets we can conclude as to why.

Answers the following questions:

  • What are category sales for (given year) versus (given year LY)?
  • What product categories are leaders?
  • What are the lowest selling product categories?

3.) Supplier In Stock vs. On Order (Worldwide)


This dashboard widget uses:

  • Suppliers->City
  • Suppliers->Country
  • Products->UnitsOnOrder
  • Products->UnitsInStock
  • Suppliers->CompanyName

This here shows the user whether demand is being met by suppliers and their location around the world. Suppliers not meeting stock are in Red, suppliers having zero stock is in yellow (again not necessarily bad, just a point of interest), and suppliers meeting or exceeding demand is in green.

Answers the following questions:

  • What suppliers are meeting or exceeding demand?
  • What suppliers are not meeting demand?
  • Is there anything to be done with suppliers having 0 orders on demand?

4.) Total Sales by American Customers


This dashboard widget uses:

  • Americas_Customer->City
  • Americas_Customer->Region
  • Americas_Customer->Country
  • Products->ProductName
  • [Order Subtotals]->Subtotal

Shown here are sales leaders in North and South America along with how diversified a client's Northwind product purchases are. This brings customer locations by city, showing where they reside and how big of a presence they have in that market. Size of the circle indicates sales, green indicates they purchase many different products, gray is an average amount, and red is small number of unique purchases.

Answers the following questions:

  • Which customers are best sellers?
  • Could high sales of one customer affect nearby customers with lower sales count?
  • Should we explore customers who buy a low amount of our different products having a need for others?
  • Are supplier issues playing any part in low sales?

5.) Total Sales by European Customers


This dashboard widget uses:

  • Europe_Customers->City
  • Europe_Customers->Region
  • Europe_Customers->Country
  • Products->ProductName
  • [Order Subtotals]->Subtotal

Similar to the American customer widget except in Europe. Looks to answer similar business questions.

Answers the following questions:

  • Which customers are best sellers?
  • Could high sales of one customer affect nearby customers with lower sales count?
  • Should we explore customers who buy a low amount of our different products having a need for others?
  • Why are many customers not buying any of our other products?
  • Are supplier issues playing any part in low sales?
